Mocking Records and Protocols

I recently wanted to test that a record was calling a function of another record in Clojure and was having a hard time figuring out how to go about the testing. I came up with a this which reminds me of mocking, although indirectly.

Generally when you define a record in Clojure, you also define a binding for accessing that record in the same namespace such as:

    
    (defprotocol A
      (afunc [this]))

    (defrecord AImpl
      (afunc [this] "a"))

    (def a (AImpl.))



If you do this for your records, one way of mocking is to redefine that binding and reify the protocol with the stubbed method.

This is what I came up with:

    ;;core-spec.clj
    (ns stubbing.core-spec
      (:use [speclj.core]
            [stubbing.core]))

    (describe "B protocol"
      (it "returns 10 as expected"
        (should= 10
                 (ten b)))

      (it "can stub A"
        (with-redefs [a (reify A
                          (return-10 [this] :stubbed))]
          (should= :stubbed
                   (ten b)))))

    ;;core.clj
    (ns stubbing.core)

    (defprotocol A
      (return-10 [this]))

    (defprotocol B
      (ten [this]))

    (defrecord AImpl []
      A
      (return-10 [this] 10))

    (def a (AImpl.))

    (defrecord BImpl []
      B
      (ten [this] (return-10 a)))

    (def b (BImpl.))



This worked out well for me. I hope anyone learning Clojure finds it useful as well.
